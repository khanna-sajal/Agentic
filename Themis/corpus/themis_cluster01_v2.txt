policy_title: Data Retention Policy Update
policy_body: |
  The updated data retention policy reinforces our commitment to data minimization while adapting to evolving internal governance and regulatory guidance. Client data must be retained for a minimum of five years after project close, as this extended timeframe provides a longer window for responding to inquiries, performing warranty obligations, and supporting historical compliance reviews. Data older than seven years shall be evaluated for deletion and, where no legal or business reason exists, it shall be purged from active storage systems.

  Under the update, retention schedules must include explicit justification for both the minimum five-year hold and the subsequent seven-year purge threshold. Data owners must conduct a documented review before the seven-year mark to determine whether certain records require longer retention due to litigation, audit, or regulatory investigation. Any records retained beyond seven years require a written exception approved by the data governance board and the privacy office. This update also introduces a requirement that retention schedules are aligned with source system classification labels, so that records subject to personal data protection rules are not retained longer than necessary. The policy remains centered on reducing unnecessary risk while maintaining sufficient retention for legitimate organizational needs.
