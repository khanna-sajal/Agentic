policy_title: GDPR Retention Amendment
policy_body: |
  UPDATED (2024 GDPR amendment): The retention policy now establishes a seven-year mandatory minimum retention period for all client data objects, reflecting the updated privacy compliance framework and the service provider's extended accountability obligations. Under this amendment, records may no longer be purged before the seven-year term is met, regardless of whether the data belongs to an inactive project or has been superseded by newer records. The amendment emphasizes that data may only be deleted after the seven-year retention period has fully elapsed and only following a documented review that confirms no continuing contractual, legal, or operational requirement exists.

  The amendment requires that retention schedules include a detailed audit trail for every deletion decision, including the rationale, authorization, and technical execution. It also introduces a requirement for quarterly retention compliance assessments, where the privacy team validates that no records subject to the seven-year policy were prematurely destroyed. Remediation actions must be taken if any early purges are discovered, including notification to senior compliance leadership and an enhanced monitoring plan for the affected business unit. The amendment underscores that any exception to the seven-year minimum term is prohibited unless explicitly permitted by a designated regulatory authority or through a formal legal hold triggered by pending litigation. This policy update creates a stricter retention model to ensure consistent long-term oversight and compliance assurance.
